Carter Perry Bailey is seeking to recruit a lawyer to join its insurance team. The role will include a varied range of work handling professional indemnity claims as well as assisting lead partners with high value and multi-party claims across a number of areas of insurance law such as life/protection insurance, Accident & Health and travel coverage, FI, D&O as well as more generic insurance and reinsurance matters (both non-contentious and contentious).

The firm is currently dealing with claims against a variety of professionals including architects, engineers, surveyors, accountants, financial professionals, insurance brokers and property professionals. In addition it is conducting a number of large and complex coverage matters.

This unique role provides the opportunity to have a wide range of experience in quality work in a collaborative environment. A good grounding in insurance principles and being a team player are key requirements. However, training shall also be provided.

The firm also offers the opportunity for a secondment to a leading claims team.

About us

Carter Perry Bailey is a specialist law firm, delivering high levels of expertise and experience with a quality of personal service rarely found in today’s marketplace. Our experience, flexibility, size, and specialisation enable us to act swiftly, proactively and efficiently to assist clients to achieve their goals.

Your role

Your role will include:

  • Reviewing documents and preparing reports
  • Complying with the SRA Code of Conduct 2011 and relevant Service Standards
  • Instructing Counsel/Experts (medical and non-medical)
  • Conducting disclosure
  • Court appointments
  • Liaising with both Insured and Insurer clients
  • Attending conferences and Trials with Counsel
  • Legal research
  • Handling routine correspondence
  • Reviewing evidence
Skills, experience and qualifications

The following experience, skills and qualifications are essential:

  • Previous defendant professional indemnity experience
  • To prioritise workload / meet deadlines
  • To pay attention to detail
  • The personal skills to deal with such work involving liaison with senior personnel, clients and third parties
  • To communicate in writing and prepare succinct reports
  • To work as part of a team
  • To work with case management systems
Personal qualities

The leading candidate will be an individual who displays:

  • Willingness to play a role in the development of client relationships and of the practice as a whole.
  • Claims handling experience (via a previous secondment or otherwise).
